## 1. Composition
- Massive angular glacier-covered mountain dominates the center and left portions of the canvas
- Dramatic exposed dark rock faces contrasted with brilliant white snow and ice
- Secondary misty mountains recede into the cloudy background
- Small evergreen trees frame the scene in lower left and right corners
- Sunlit green meadow provides warm contrast in the immediate foreground
- Deep crevasses and flowing ice patterns cascade down the glacier's face
- Atmospheric clouds weave between peaks creating depth and mystery

Composition archetype: **Alpine Glacier Monument**

## 2. Palette
- **Phthalo Blue**: Dominant in mountain shadows, glacier depths, and sky tones
- **Prussian Blue**: Deep shadows in exposed rock and darkest crevasses
- **Midnight Black**: Sharp rock faces and evergreen tree forms
- **Titanium White**: Brilliant snow coverage, ice highlights, and cloud masses
- **Sap Green**: Meadow grass and evergreen foliage
- **Van Dyke Brown**: Tree trunks and mountain rock details
- **Yellow Ochre**: Warm meadow highlights in foreground
- **Indian Yellow**: Sunlit grass creating warmth against the cold glacier

Palette identity: **Ice Blue Majesty**

## 3. Mood & Atmosphere
- Powerful and imposing presence of ancient ice
- Cold, pristine alpine environment with crystalline clarity
- Mysterious and ethereal quality in the misty background mountains
- Dramatic contrast between harsh glacial landscape and welcoming meadow
- Sense of scale, isolation, and natural grandeur
- Serene yet formidable atmosphere

Lighting: **High Alpine Clarity**

## 4. Structural Layout
- **Left**: Dark evergreen trees anchor the foreground; glacier's dramatic slope dominates
- **Right**: Evergreen trees and glimpse of background mountain through mist
- **Center**: The towering glacier mountain commands attention with its angular presence
- **Foreground**: Sunlit green meadow provides warm, accessible entry point
- **Midground**: Massive glacier with exposed rock and flowing ice patterns
- **Background**: Misty secondary peaks dissolve into atmospheric clouds

Depth style: **Layered Alpine Recession**

## 5. Motion
- Glacier appears to flow downward with dramatic diagonal lines and crevasse patterns
- Clouds drift horizontally across background peaks creating atmospheric movement
- The angular tilt of the main mountain creates dynamic tension and implied motion
- Eye flows from warm foreground meadow up the glacier face to misty peaks
- Vertical evergreens provide counterpoint to the diagonal glacier flow

Motion profile: **Cascading Ice Flow**

## 6. Technique
- **Palette knife**: Heavy application for angular rock faces and glacier structure
- **Knife pulling**: Creating snow highlights and ice flow patterns with decisive strokes
- **Fan brush**: Evergreen trees with characteristic downward strokes
- **Wet-on-wet blending**: Atmospheric mist and cloud integration with mountains
- **Layering**: Building depth through progressive mountain layers
- **Scraping and lifting**: Exposing sharp edges and crevasse details in glacier
- **Cross-hatching strokes**: Creating texture in the meadow grass

Signature technique: **Angular Knife-Work Glacier Formation**

## 7. Narrative Layer
"An ancient river of ice commands the alpine landscape, its blue-shadowed crevasses and brilliant white surface testament to countless winters. The warm meadow below offers a moment of gentle welcome before the harsh majesty of the glacier, while ghost-like peaks emerge from clouds beyond, suggesting vast wilderness stretching to the horizon. This is nature's cathedral of ice and stone, where time moves in millennia and human presence fades to insignificance."

## 8. Initial Canvas Treatment
- Canvas prepared with Liquid White base for sky and background areas
- Possible light blue pre-toning in upper portions to establish cool atmospheric foundation
- Wet surface allowing for seamless blending of clouds and misty mountain transitions
- The wet base enables soft atmospheric effects while supporting sharp knife work for glacier

Initial treatment creates the foundation for both soft atmospheric depth and crisp glacial definition.
